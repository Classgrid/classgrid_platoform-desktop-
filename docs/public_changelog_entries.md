Title: Smarter Support and Inquiry Management
Type: feature
Module: support
Summary: Classgrid now gives users, institutions, and public visitors a more organized way to raise questions, share details, and track support conversations from one place.
Content:
Classgrid's support experience has been upgraded into a more complete and professional request-management flow for both existing platform users and new public inquiries. Instead of treating every message like a simple contact form, the platform now supports a more organized conversation journey where users can submit a request, attach useful context, continue the discussion, and receive updates as the request moves forward. Public visitors can use Classgrid Talk to raise inquiries without needing a full platform login, while institutions can include their organization name so the team understands the background of the request from the beginning. The experience is designed to keep conversations connected, which means users can reply to an existing request instead of repeatedly starting from zero or creating duplicate messages. Behind the scenes, support teams can manage each request by its current status, priority, category, and ownership, giving the team a clearer view of what needs attention and who is responsible for the next step. If a request has been marked resolved but the user still needs help, it can be reopened so the conversation continues naturally. Satisfaction feedback can also be collected after the interaction, helping Classgrid understand whether the response was actually useful. Email updates keep users informed when a request is created or answered, reducing uncertainty while they wait for support. Overall, this makes the Classgrid support experience feel more reliable, traceable, and customer-friendly, while also giving the internal support team a stronger workflow for handling issues and inquiries with consistency.

Title: End-to-End Admissions Workflow
Type: feature
Module: admissions
Summary: Classgrid now supports a complete admissions journey, helping institutions manage applications, documents, merit processes, payments, enrollment, and parent visibility in one connected system.
Content:
The admissions module has grown into a complete operational workflow for managing student intake from the first application stage through final enrollment. Institutions can now handle secure candidate access, application drafts, detailed student information, document collection, verification, merit-based selection, seat movement, payment-related steps, and enrollment actions inside one connected process. This is especially useful for schools, junior colleges, coaching institutes, and professional colleges that need more than a basic enquiry form because admission work often involves identity checks, academic details, family information, eligibility rules, document review, fee confirmation, and multiple rounds of decision-making. Students can begin an application, save progress, and continue later, which makes longer forms easier to complete without losing information. Admission teams can review applications, verify documents, move students through structured stages, manage waitlists, allocate seats, assign divisions, and complete enrollment with better visibility. Merit workflows help institutions handle competitive admissions more transparently, especially when ranking, eligibility, category, seat availability, and round movement matter. Parent-facing tracking gives families clearer visibility into the application status, reducing repeated calls and manual follow-ups. The platform also supports bulk admission operations such as verification, selection, compliance updates, round advancement, and structured exports for administrative reporting. This brings admissions away from scattered spreadsheets, manual notes, disconnected payment checks, and repeated phone coordination. For institutions, the benefit is a more controlled and professional admission pipeline; for students and parents, the benefit is a clearer journey with better updates and fewer confusing gaps between application, verification, selection, payment, and enrollment.

Title: Institution Website Publishing
Type: feature
Module: websites
Summary: Classgrid now allows institutions to manage and publish their own public website content directly from the platform, with structured sections for branding, admissions, notices, programs, gallery, events, and contact information.
Content:
Classgrid now includes a website management experience that helps institutions maintain a professional public presence directly from the platform. Each institution can prepare its own public-facing content, keep it in draft mode while it is being edited, and publish it only when the information is ready to be shown to visitors. This gives schools, colleges, and coaching institutes more control over their digital identity without depending on a separate website team for every routine update. Institution teams can manage branding, colors, hero content, program details, admission information, notices, faculty profiles, gallery media, fee details, result highlights, events, contact information, governance content, academic details, downloads, and other public sections in a structured way. Notices can be used for admissions, exams, holidays, events, and general announcements, giving students and parents a clearer place to find official updates. Gallery sections allow institutions to present campus life, facilities, achievements, events, and culture through images and videos, making the public website feel more active and trustworthy. The structured content approach keeps information organized instead of spread across disconnected manual pages, which makes maintenance easier for non-technical teams. Institution-specific branding also helps each site feel official, recognizable, and aligned with the organization's identity. Public visitors can see the correct institution content automatically once the site is published, while platform administrators can review website availability across institutions from one place. This feature strengthens Classgrid's role as more than an internal ERP system by giving institutions a public communication layer that supports admissions, reputation, updates, and day-to-day digital presence.

Title: Digital Canteen Ordering
Type: feature
Module: canteen
Summary: Classgrid now supports digital canteen operations with menu management, student ordering, online payment flow, live kitchen tracking, pickup status updates, order history, ratings, and sales insights.
Content:
The canteen module brings daily food ordering and kitchen coordination into the Classgrid platform, helping institutions manage campus food service in a more organized and transparent way. Instead of relying only on manual counters, paper tokens, and crowded queues, institutions can maintain a digital menu with item names, prices, categories, images, availability, dietary tags, and stock limits. Daily specials can be configured for selected days, allowing the canteen to promote rotating meals, limited-time items, and planned offers more consistently. Students can place orders digitally from their account, and item availability is checked before the order is accepted, which helps reduce confusion around out-of-stock items. Successful orders move into a live kitchen queue so staff can clearly see what needs to be prepared, and each order receives a pickup token that makes collection easier during busy periods. Kitchen staff can move orders through stages such as new, preparing, ready, delivered, or cancelled, keeping the order lifecycle clear for both staff and students. When an order is ready, the student can be updated so they know when to collect it instead of waiting unnecessarily near the counter. Students can also view their previous canteen orders and rate purchased items after completion, helping institutions understand food quality, preferences, and demand patterns. Canteen managers get visibility into daily revenue and top-selling items, which supports better planning, stock decisions, and menu improvements. Overall, this module improves speed, reduces queue confusion, gives students a smoother ordering experience, and gives institutions a more modern way to manage canteen operations.
